So I have this one very crisp and distinct memory of 9th grade biology, wherein my teacher told us that both men and women have hormone cycles. He then said that the only reason that we don't notice it in men is because men don't have periods. In my little 14 year old mind I was like,

Yeah, that checks out.

It did not check out.

For whatever reason, this "fact" just never left my mind. Cut to seven years later when I was an employed scientist, and this interaction happened:

Labmate with menstrual pains: Who's stupid ass idea was it to make women's hormones cycle up and down all the time.

Me, before I learned that being a know-it-all is a bad look: Well actually, men have hormone cycles too! We just never really notice it.

That... doesn't sound right, Kat.

N-no no, it's like basic biology. I'd bet money on this.

Why would men need to have a hormone cycle? All they ever do is make sperm. They don't have periodic reproductive behaviour.

Well, I googled it and she was right.

"So," I hear you ask, "your 9th grade biology teacher just straight up lied to you and that's what you came here to yell into the void about?" No, of course not. How dare you. Mr. Jones was a sweet and lovely angel who was merely the victim of misinformation that unscrupulous scientists in the 1970s unleashed upon the world for no God damned apparent reason. MR. JONES AND I WERE LIED TO BY THE ESTABLISHMENT AND I WOULD LIKE SOME RECKONING.

Basically, in 1975, the least impressive occurence of peer review in history happened when this paper got published. It supposedly "confirmed" the male hormone cycle by showing that blood testosterone levels fluctuate periodically in, like, some dudes. The study looked at 20 guys, of whom 12 seemed to have changes in blood testosterone levels over the course of two months, and the other 8 had no detectable changes. So basically, there was no evidence that almost half the men had any kind of hormone cycle at all. You know what would happen if you did the same study on sexually mature people with uteruses?

THEY WOULD ALL HAVE CYCLING HORMONE LEVELS.

But like... why is this random study from almost 50 years ago so apparently influential? Welp. Turns out, scientists just love to fucking lie about the random, obscure, claim that men have a hormone cycle.
