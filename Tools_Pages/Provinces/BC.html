Bur chervil - Anthriscus caucalis

Idenitifcation

Bur chervil ranges in height from 1 -2 m tall. Clusters of 3-7 small white flowers sit atop 2 cm long stalks. Leaves are fern-like, triangular, and hairy. The seeds are approximately 4 mm long with distinctly hooked prickles.

Edible Parts

The entire plant is edible. Leaves taste like mild, licorice-y, parsley. They are very delicate, and lose flavour on cooking; it is best to use them as a garnish. Chervil is in the carrot family, so the root can be cooked with other vegetables.

Cautions

Chervils are closely related to poisonous plants. Take great care with identification, and DO NOT eat a chervil unless you are certain you have IDed it properly.

Canada Thistle - Cirsium arvense

Idenitifcation

It grows 0.3 to 2.0 m tall, has shiny green leaves with sharp, spiny edges and has white hairs on the leafâ€™s underside. It has clusters of up to five pompom-like flowers on stem tips with a faint vanilla scent. Seeds look like a dandelion i.e., parachute-type seeds. You can tell Canada thistle from all similar species by its smooth main stem and small flowers.

Edible Parts

The entire plant is edible, but thorns must be removed before eating. Leaves can be eaten like spinach, and roots taste of lettuce. Stems are best eaten before flowers appear, otherwise they are stringy and hollow.Peeled flowers are edible and similar to artichokes, but generally considered "not worth the effort."

Cautions

All thistles are edible, but ensure that you do not harvest native thistle species, which look similar.
